Here's a timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England.
  • 1931 - Attends King's College at the University of Cambridge.
  • 1934 - Awarded first-class honours in mathematics.
  • 1935 - Elected a Fellow of King's College due to a dissertation in which he proved the central limit theorem.
  • 1938 - Obtains his PhD from the Department of Mathematics at Princeton University.
  • 1938 - Begins working part-time at the GC&CS (now GCHQ). With Dilly Knox, Turing focused on cryptanalysis of the Enigma cipher machine used by Nazi Germany.
  • 1939 - Turing reports to Bletchley Park, GC&CS's wartime station, the day after UK had declared war on Germany.
  • 1939 - Designs the "bombe", an electro-mechanical device that would go on to help decipher many encrypted German messages made using the enigma machine.
  • 1941 - Proposes to colleague Joan Clarke, but after admitting to her his homosexuality, decides he could not go through with the marriage.
  • 1945 - Is awarded an OBE for his work during the war.
  • 1952 - Starts a relationship with Arnold Murray. As homosexuality was a crime in the UK at the time, Turing was charged for "gross indecency" and was required to undergo hormonal physical changes.
  • 1954 - Turing is found dead of cyanide poisoning, with an inquest determining the cause of death as suicide.
